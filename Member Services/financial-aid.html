Financial Aid

Helping students and families participate in the NCC

Vision

The NCC believes that no child who truly wants to engage in singing and music education should be prevented from doing so due to financial hardship. Our Financial Aid program is in place to help families with financial need be a part of our program, while simultaneously advancing our mission of inclusivity and diversity.

Procedures

  • During the application process for membership in the NCC, a family will express the desire for financial assistance.
  • Upon successful completion of the audition and final interview, the family is sent a link to complete an online application for financial aid
  • Applicants must pay the NCC registration fee of $95 or $195 concurrently with filling out their financial aid materials. This may be waived in rare cases for a family in very dire financial circumstances (annual income below $10,000).
  • A deadline for all financial aid applications in a given season is set by the NCC Operations Manager.
  • Once the deadline has passed, the Operations Manager reviews all applications and imports data from the applications into a dedicated spreadsheet for Financial Aid award tracking. The spreadsheet is stored in the NCC Dropbox under NCC Executives/Financial Aid.
  • The Operations Manager schedules a phone meeting with the Executive Director or CEO to present the Financial Aid application data.
  • Each application is given individual review. Financial need is determined through an assessment of the family’s annual income as reported on their most recently filed tax return, as well as a statement of any extraordinary circumstances which may affect current expenses, such as a catastrophic employment loss, illness or other hardship.
  • Proof of current annual income must be on file for each applicant. If the most recent tax return is not reflective of current income (due to job loss or other circumstances) then pay stubs or other supporting documentation must be collected to support the annual income number.
  • Awards are disbursed according to the following basic rubric:
Annual Income Tuition Percentage Subsidization
0–$30,000 55%–75%
$30,000–$60,000 35%–55%
$60,000–$90,000 20%–35%
$90,000–above No award offered (unless extraordinary and catastrophic circumstances)
  • The above guidelines are general in nature, and individual consideration is given to each applicant for unique circumstances. For example, if annual income was $75,000 but an illness in the family had caused $40,000 in medical expenses during the past year, a family could be given an award for the $35,000 annual income bracket if they can provide documentation supporting the medical expenses.
  • The Operations Manager and Executive Director/CEO jointly determine the award amount for each applicant and enter it into the Dropbox spreadsheet.
  • The Operations Manager sends notification to Financial Aid recipients by customizing an e-mail template for each person.
  • The Member Services Department tracks sign-ups for financial aid tuition accounts and ensures all accounts have been completed before the first rehearsal of the upcoming season.

Results

A successful financial aid program will have the following results:

  • The family in need will feel that they are paying a considerable yet manageable amount for NCC tuition. We do not want a family to feel that they are getting something for nothing. The right amount for their award is an amount that will make them able to participate in the program, while also making them value the services of the organization.
  • The NCC membership will be socio-economically diverse, and at least 5% of its students will be recipients of need-based financial aid.
  • Good will, enthusiasm, and gratitude will be generated in financial aid recipients.
  • Donors and foundations will give additional funding to the organization specifically to support the financial aid program.

FAQs

  • Does the NCC help financial aid recipients cover the cost of anything besides tuition, such as the NCC uniform, NCC T-shirt or music scores?

    As a general rule, financial aid is only given to offset the cost of tuition, and recipients must still pay the registration fee of $95 or $195, as well as the cost of the uniform and seasonal music. In rare cases, we will make an exception and waive the registration fee, music fees or NCC t-shirt cost for families in dire cases whose annual income is below $10,000 per year. The cost of the formal uniform cannot be waived since it is purchased from a third-party vendor. However, to help with this, the NCC can sometimes facilitate a Uniform Exchange event where used uniform pieces are available to families for $5 each.

  • Does the NCC offer financial aid for the International Tour?

    Yes. Any student who has received financial aid for NCC tuition may receive the same aid for the cost of the international tour. For example, if a student is receiving a 30% tuition subsidization, they may also be subsidized for 30% of the cost of a tour package. This only applies to the tour package for the student; if a parent accompanies the student, s/he must pay full price for the tour.
