Selecting Repertoire v0.1.0

Choosing music to develop skills and encourage joyful expression

Vision

Repertoire selection plays a key role in the vocal development of NCC students. The goal is to provide musical selections which offer variety and versatility for optimal growth, experience and exposure.

Procedures

Timing

Repertoire submissions are due a minumum of two (2) months prior to the start of Fall or Spring.

How to Submit

  • Submit Full PDF scores of your selections to Dr. Blackstone via e-mail by or before the due date. Feel free to write "Perusal Copy" on the PDF. In order to be considered, we must see the entire score to view the vocal range, length and quality of the selection.
  • Please do not send a link to a partial perusal copy.
  • Please supply us with the directly link for purchase from J.W. Pepper.
  • It is important to have the exact link for purchase so that there are no mistakes when ordering your ensemble's scores.
  • List your selections in the following format:​
    • Sarasponda - Ruth Boshkoff, 2-part
    • Tum Balalaika - Lois Brownsey and Marti Lunn Lantz, 2-part

Musical Guideline

Your musical set should consist of:

  • At least one (1) foreign language piece.
  • Varied tempos, keys, colors, moods, styles.
  • Classical, folk, modern, jazz or Broadway selections.
  • A vocal range appropriate for teaching head voice in the bel canto style.
  • The vocal range should not extend below middle C (C4) and should include notes up through high F or G (F5 or G5).
  • If a piece you are interested in sites too low in the register, please find a higher arrangement or have it transposed to a higher key.

Number of Pieces per NCC Ensemble

  • Prelude Level - three (3) selections per season.
  • Minuet Level - three (3) to four (4) selections per season.
  • Sonata Level - four (4) selections per season.
  • Concerto Level - four (4) to five (5) selections.

Results

With a well-selected repertoire set, the students should experience the following:

  • Joy and passion for their music.
  • A desire to execute the music with precision and expression.
  • A well-developed and beautifli head tone.
  • ​Focused, brliliant, open, flil and free.
  • No chest voice singing.
  • ​Excitement for foreign language pieces.
  • The abliity to sing a wide range of musical styles.

FAQs

  • What if a score is not available at jwpepper.com?

    Please include a link to sheetmusicplus.com. If the score cannot be found there, you may send a link to any music company which sells the octavo.

  • What if I like a piece, but cannot find an arrangement?

    In order for a piece to be approved, we must be able to purchase octavos of the score for your ensemble. If an arrangement is not available, you may need to find another musical selection.

  • What if a piece is in the public domain?

    Please If a piece is public domain, you may use it if you are able to locate a well-printed original score. An original score is not, for example, a song from the 24 Italian Songs and Arias book published by Schirmer, as this arrangment is owned by Schirmer.

    Public domain site is imslp.org for verification.
