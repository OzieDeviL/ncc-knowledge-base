Voice Instructors Rehearsal Protocol

The importance of individual instruction

. Vision

The focus of the private lessons is to teach proper vocal technique, including breath support, resonance, placement of tone and vowel formation. Please assist the students in achieving the best possible tone quality while addressing any vocal faults or habits.

. Procedures

Arrival

  • Voice instructors should be in place no later than fifteen (15) minutes prior to the beginning of any given session, so that the lesson space can be adequately prepared, and so that the first student is received in a timely and organized manner.
  • In an emergency situation where you are running late, please make sure to notify Chelsea Morel at (760) 831-4505.
  • If you are providing a substitute for the day, please provide the substitute’s contact information (e-mail and phone) at least one (1) to two (2) weeks prior to the date of your absence. This information should be e-mailed to Dr. Blackstone.

Lesson Administration

  • Lessons are administered in ten (10) minute intervals, and must be kept perfectly on schedule (otherwise students at the end are denied their lesson, causing possible disappointment, and lessening the productivity of an instructor’s time at the NCC).
  • This means that no matter what time a student arrives for his or her session, it must end exactly on time.  For instance, in Los Angeles, our first sessions of the day look like this:â€‹
    • 1:00–1:10 PM - Student #1
    • 1:10–1:20 PM - Student #2
    • 1:20–1:30 PM - Student #3
    • 1:30–1:40 PM - Student #4
    • 1:40–1:50 PM - Student #5
  • Student #1 is sent to the lesson space at 1:00 PM. He or she may arrive at 1:01.
  • The lesson starts, and as soon as the clock turns 1:10, he or she is sent back to rehearsal.
  • Once Student #1 returns to rehearsal, Student #2 automatically stands, and proceeds to the lesson space.
  • Student #2 may arrive in the lesson space at 1:11. Still, his or her lesson must end at 1:20. We just want to be clear that it is the end-time that must be precise in order to keep the students on schedule.

. Possible Problems and Solutions

Please feel free to communicate anything distinctive that is noticed in the private lessons with the directors by e-mail. At the NCC, we really try to work together as a team to identify the unique needs of every student, and meet them with a unified approach. We are very interested in what is discovered in the private sessions, and would love any applicable feedback.
