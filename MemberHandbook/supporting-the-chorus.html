. Conductor’s Circle

Making It Happen

The Conductor’s Circle is the NCC’s annual member donor program, designed to help support our educational mission while offering perks both fun and valuable to our patrons without whose help we could not exist. Read through the list of contribution levels below and decide which one is right for you! Getting friends and family involved is yet another important way to help maintain the quality of our curriculum. Recruit as many members as you can! The education for which the NCC stands is absolutely worth it.

Donation Levels

Donor status is calculated based on total donations received within the last or current calendar year.

Associate - $100

  • Year-round concert program and website recognition.
  • Monthly E-Newsletter.

Supporter - $500

  • All benefits listed above, plus two (2) tickets to an NCC rehearsal.

Friend - $1,000

  • All benefits listed above, plus two (2) tickets to a subscription performance Dress Rehearsal.

Sponsor - $1,500

  • All benefits listed above, plus Founder recognition in programs and on NCC website for life.

Patron - $2,500

  • All benefits listed above, plus two (2) tickets to the NCC Annual Awards Ceremony.

Benefactor - $5,000

  • All benefits listed above, plus two (2) tickets to the NCC Annual Fundraiser.

Guardian - $10,000

  • All benefits listed above, plus special recognition on the title page of the NCC seasonal concert pogram, and a premium dining experience with Artistic Director Luke McEndarfer and Executive Director Cristina Demiany.

Champion - $25,000

  • All benefits listed above, plus a private event performance by the NCC Scholars in Los Angeles, or the Premier Ensemble in New York.

The NCC is a 501(c)3 non-profit corporation. All donations to the National Children’s Chorus are 100% tax-deductible.

Spreading the Word

We ask that all NCC families are fans of the National Children’s Chorus on Fcebook. We also ask that each parent suggests to friends and family that they should become fans as well! It’s fun and easy! Here’s how:

  1. Sign in to Facebook.
  2. Go to the National Children’s Chorus Fan Page by entering “National Children’s Chorus” in the “search” field and pressing enter, or by typing the following link into your browser: www.facebook.com/nationalchildrenschorus.
  3. Click “Like” if you are not already a fan.

All fans will receive regular updates about concerts and events in their news feeds, as well as various happenings at the NCC and access to new photo albums, videos and soundtracks.

Keeping an Eye on Student Safety

While it is vitally important that the NCC uses all common marketing methods for promoting itself (such as Facebook and other online marketing tools), it is also imperative that we uphold strict safety standards to protect our young musicians’ identities, as well as discourage personal memberships to online social communities until they are of appropriate age, or have proper parental consent with applicable supervision. For these reasons, the NCC mandates the following policies in regards to Facebook and other online media:

  • While the images of students at events will be published to the general pblic, only their first names will be listed, if any at all. Last names will not be listed.
  • The NCC does not encourage or condone that students should join Fcebook or any other adult social media network, and accepts no responsibility for any circumstance that may arise as a result of a sudent who does so.
  • Should a child be permitted to have an online social media account, NCC Artistic and Administrative Team members are forbidden to engage in any messaging or chatting with a student online and are not alowed to approve him or her as a “friend” or “contact” until the cild is no longer a minor (18 years of age or older).
