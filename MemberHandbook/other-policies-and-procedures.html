. NCC Solo Audition Policies & Procedures

NCC Solo Roles

Students in the National Children’s Chorus are given the opportunity to audition for solo roles as they naturally come up within the course of the performance season. Solo assignments are based on skill level and determined by the needs of the musical production as deemed appropriate by the ensemble’s director, or a panel of judges. Not every student will qualify to receive a solo assignment, and some students may qualify for several. It must be clearly understood that the NCC is a chorus (not a solo studio), and singing solo at an NCC event is not a guaranteed experience. With that in mind, the NCC ardently supports the vocal development of each singer and enthusiastically offers the right solo experiences for students when they become ready.

Solo Eligibility

  • Students must be present for the official audition as set by the director in order to be considered. Make-up auditions are not permitted.
  • Students already exceeding the maximum number of absences for a given season are not permitted to audition. Students exceeding the maximum number of absences allowed for a given season after a solo has been awarded to him or her will be relieved of the solo which will then be reassigned to another student, unless there is special director approval.
  • Behavioral misconduct prior to an audition will render a student ineligible. Behavioral misconduct after a solo has been awarded will result in the solo role’s being taken away from the student and reassigned to another student.
  • Soloists must attend all solo coaching sessions and every rehearsal of Tech Week (listed on the schedule), including the Dress Rehearsal. An absence at any of these practices will result in the solo’s being reassigned, unless there is special director approval.

. Audition Feedback

At the NCC, it our goal to give useful and constructive information in helping our students identify ways to improve their performance. E-mail correspondence may be made with any of our directors to get requested feedback on the strengths and weaknesses of any particular audition, where advice will be willingly given on how to improve chances of securing a solo role in the future.

. Letters of Recommendation

Promoting the Excellence of our Students

Directors of the NCC are happy to assist with applications to schools and other music programs as they come up throughout the year.

Respecting the Time of our Professionals

While our directors are fully willing to donate their time in helping you and your child, we ask that you give them a minimum of 30 days advance notice for any such request, no matter how big or small. As our instructors are very busy professionals, lastminute, stressful requests put a strain on their schedules, and therefore cannot be allowed. For this reason, any request made within 30 days may be declined. As it is our wish to support your children in the best way possible, we kindly ask that you enable us to do so by submitting your request in a timely manner.

Utilizing Office Hours and Letterhead

Though our instructors donate their time to write your child a letter of recommendation, or fill out a form, our office that processes the letter and assists with the administrative aspect of seeing that it is submitted on time comes at an added, hourly cost to the organization. Since some students do not ever ask for recommendation processing, and others ask for several letters, it is only fair that we charge separately for this service. The fees for office processing of the following documents are as follows:

  • Form to be filled out by director and mailed in (no letterhead used) - $35 per form.
  • Letter to be composed by director, printed on letterhead and mailed in - $65 per letter, $35 per duplicate copy going to seperate recipient.
  • Reduced fees for students on financial aid will be assessed on an as-need basis. Please contact our office for more information.

Important Note

All requests for letters of recommendation must be made via the NCC’s online form, available at My NCC. Directors shall not begin work on any recommendation project until the form and payment have been submitted. For students on financial aid, please follow the prompts on the form to request financial assistance with this endeavor.

. Gifts to Directors

Showing Your Appreciation…

Policy

We are so honored that you would want to present a gift to one of our Artistic and Administrative Team members. The NCC permits such gestures of goodwill on an individual basis only. Group collections of cash and/or other goods without the express knowledge and consent of the Executive Director is strictly forbidden. Please make sure that all gifts given to NCC Team members are done so on a one-to-one personal basis only. If you are unsure or have any questions regarding this policy, please contact the Executive Director.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I buy a card and have several people sign it, presenting it to my child’s director?

Yes.

Can I take a small collection and use the money to buy flowers for the director?

No. Cash collections may not be taken on behalf of the NCC. NCC parents must not be solicted by other parents for contributions.

Can I present the director with my own gift card and/ or present?

Yes. Individual gifts are allowed.

As always, NCC Artistic and Administrative Team members consider their work with your children the greatest gift of all, and have no need for anything additional. However, if it is your wish to express added appreciation by presenting one of our Team members with a gift, it is both welcomed and appreciated.

. Regional Audition Representatives

Helping Us Make a Spectacular Impression

One time per season, the NCC holds auditions for new members, both in Los Angeles and New York. For these events, in addition to testing the skills of prospective singers, the NCC introduces both new parents and students to the organization by way of interaction with our own NCC members. If your child is selected to represent the NCC at one of our regional auditions, you will be contacted by NCC Administration. Thereafter, once the engagement has been agreed upon, he or she will be expected to adhere to the following—

  • Must arrive 15 minutes prior to the beginning of the audition start time.
  • Must be dressed in NCC Formal Dress, including all grooming requirements.
  • Must meet and greet prospective parents and students.
  • Must participate in the audition posture and vocalizing demonstration.
  • Must sing a piece of music, or speak a few words about the NCC experience.

Should your child be selected and participate as an NCC Regional Audition Representative, he or she will receive special recognition at the Awards Ceremony at the end of the year.

. Golden Apple Awards

Excellence must be recognized!

Each month (October through May) a single student from each ensemble will receive the coveted Golden Apple Award. During the process of rehearsing each month, directors keep track of performance excellence by awarding points for good behavior, superb musicianship, participation and improvement. The student with the greatest achievement for each month shall be honored with a special award (limit one per student per performance year, multiple months as winner not allowed). Additionally, he or she is given recognition for this distinction at the Awards Ceremony at the end of the year, along with a special certificate.
