New York

The Church of St. Paul the Apostle

Corner of Columbus Avenue & West 60th Street New York, NY 10019

The Church of St. Paul the Apostle was constructed between 1876 and 1885, and its interior architectural style is inspired by 4th & 5th century early Christian basilicas in Ravenna, Italy. The grand exterior of the church features 13th century Gothic elements, streamlined by early American modernism. The acoustics of the church are world-renowned, and boast a six-second echo which favors high, pure sounds such as those made by the National Children’s Chorus.

. Box Office Policies

Get Your Tickets

Tickets to all NCC events are for sale online 24 hours a day, 7 days a week up until 1:00 PM the day before the show. Beyond that point, tickets must be purchased at the door, if still available. To purchase your tickets, visit www.nationalchildrenschorus.com.

No Refunds

All NCC ticket purchases are final and non-refundable.

No Exchanges

All NCC ticket purchases are valid only for the event and time that was originally ordered. Ticket exchanges between separate events, or between different show times within the same event, are not permitted.

No Coordinated Reselling of Your Tickets Through the NCC

Tickets may not be resold, and the NCC will not facilitate the reselling of any purchased tickets.

Donating Your Tickets Back to the NCC

Tickets that are not going to be used may be donated back to the NCC in exchange for a letter acknowledging the full value of your donation for tax purposes. Ticket donations do not qualify for membership or upgrades within the NCC’s Conductor’s Circle donor program.

Children Under 5

No children under five (5) years of age will be admitted to an NCC Subscription Concert Event (please check your Season Subscription to see which events are listed).

Children under 5 will be permitted to attend any and all NCC events that are not a part of the Season Subscription.

Patrons of all ages to all NCC events must hold a purchased ticket for admittance. Children are not permitted to sit on the laps of adults in lieu of purchasing a seat, and must sit in the seat indicated on their ticket.

. Late Seating and Re-entry at Concerts

Policy

Once a performance has begun, seating will not be permitted until a designated “late seating” opportunity, or until intermission.

Even if a concert guest leaves the hall to use the bathroom during the performance, he or she will not be reseated until intermission. This is standard procedure at any professional concert hall, and will apply to all NCC events.

Respecting Our Performers

Nothing is more distracting for a performer during a concert than noises or activity from the audience. As our singers have spent months working to perfect their skills, disruptions from the audience due to latecomers cannot be allowed.

Respecting Our Patrons

Nothing is more distracting for an audience member during a concert than noises or activity from other audience members. As our patrons have purchased a ticket in order to partake in a quality music experience, it is our responsibility to make sure that their expectation is fulfilled. Therefore, late seating cannot be allowed. Please urge all your concert guests to be on time.

. No Photography or Video at Concert Events

Policy

Photography/video during any NCC musical performance or rehearsal is not permitted.

Respecting Our Performers and Audience Members

At the NCC, we are heavily invested in providing the highest quality musical experience for both our performers and audience members alike. Therefore, any activities by concert patrons that could potentially take away from the beauty of the music are strictly forbidden. The clicking and flashing of cameras, the holding up of camcorders, and the overall distracting energy of a person who is taking a picture/video rather than listening to the music are all things we strongly want to avoid. Be in the present moment! The gift of live music is that it cannot be experienced at any other time the same way. Don’t spoil it by doing anything else other than enjoying the gorgeous artistry your children have worked so hard to create.

Don’t Worry—We’ve Got You Covered…

  • All NCC performances are professionally recorded by a videographer, and are made available to NCC Members within a few weeks of each event.
  • Personal pictures may be taken after any event with the director and fellow NCC members in the grand lobby or in the actual concert space, depending on the venue.

Other Steps You Can Take to Keep the Concert Focused on the Music…

We recommend that concert patrons sit as quietly as possible during the performance. Try to refrain from:

  • Talking or whispering.
  • Moving around.
  • Noisily unwrapping cough drops or candy.
  • Texting.

Again, take advantage of the rare opportunity to be completely 100% focused on your children’s beautiful singing. It is a precious moment in time that will not last, so let go of the distractions and engage it fully!

The Broad Stage

. The Big Day

You have practiced and prepared for months, you have refined your vocal technique, and you are ready to deliver a stunning performance. We are already so proud of you! Here are some important things to remember that will help make your concert experience as enjoyable and successful as possible!

The Night Before

  • Eat a nutritious dinner.
  • Get to bed early and sleep for a minimum of eight (8) hours.
  • Stay well-hydrated.

The Day of the Show

  • Eat nutritious meals, drink water and make sure you are well-fed before your arrival at the concert venue.
  • Review your music once at home, so all important performance directions are fresh in your mind.
  • Stay mentally focused—do not watch TV, listen to music, text friends, talk on the phone—simply relax.
  • Do not use your voice—conserve it for the performance; absolutely no shouting, screaming or loud talking. Remember not to whisper either, as it dries out the voice.
  • If possible, leave plenty of time to get ready, so you do not feel rushed.
  • Make sure you are in full compliance with the NCC Uniform and grooming requirements.

Before leaving home—do not forget

  • Your music.
  • Any pertinent NCC Uniform pieces that you are not currently wearing, including vest, shoes, socks, stockings.
  • Snack and a bottle of water.

Arriving at the Concert Venue

  • Make sure to enter from the Artist Entrance or designated meeting point for performers.
  • All students must be in place fifteen (15) minutes prior to the call time—be early!
