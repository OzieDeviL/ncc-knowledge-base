. Vision

Each year, the National Children’s Chorus must file an annual tax return, which is designated IRS Form 990 . (officially, the “Return of Organization Exempt from Income Tax”). This is a United States Internal Revenue Service form that provides the public with financial information about a nonprofit organization. Filing this document in the public record ensures transparency in non-profit financial procedures to make sure that funds are managed appropriately. Completing the annual tax return is required by law and must be completed within certain time parameters. This important project is a critical component of our company’s financial procedures; grant applications for funding are not possible without a current 990 on file in the public record.

. Procedures

  • When the NCC’s fiscal year concludes on the 30 th of June each year, Missionwell Accounting Firm will finalize all financial reports for that fiscal year. The financial reports must be checked by a controller and all revenue and expenses verified as correct before the books can be closed on the fiscal year.
  • Once all financials for the fiscal year are finalized, the independent auditing firm Rose, Snyder and Jacobs should be contacted by e-mail in August or early September to open discussion on the annual audit. The audit will be worked on between September and December, in partnership with Missionwell.
  • Filing the 990 is handled by Rose, Snyder and Jacobs. They should be asked to provide a quote for this work in August.
  • Form 990 must be filed by the 15th day of the 5th month after the end of our organization’s accounting period. Thus, for the NCC’s fiscal year ending June 30th, Form 990 is due November 15th of the same year.
  • Due to the timing of completing the audit, we must file an extension on Form 990. We use IRS Form 8868 . to request an automatic 3-month extension of time to file Form 990 and also to apply for an additional (not automatic) 3-month extension if needed.
  • Filing the extension is handled by Rose, Snyder and Jacobs, with Caryn Ryan at Missionwell copied on the information.
  • Our contact for the tax return at RSJ is Jeni Park.
  • In early January, Jeni Park should be contacted to check in on the tax return. Matt Domanski at Missionwell should be copied on this correspondence. Jeni will use the audited financial statements to complete the tax return.
  • Jeni will send a list of items to NCC and Missionwell that are needed for the tax return.
  • NCC will need to provide a spreadsheet list of all single donations of $5,000 or higher received during the fiscal year. The spreadsheet must include:
    • Donor Name
    • Donor Address
    • Address Type (business or personal)
    • Date of Donation
    • Donation Amount
    • Payment Method
  • Jeni will send a completed draft of the tax return to NCC for review.
  • If approved, Jeni will send an e-mail with instructions on how to sign, e-file and mail the tax return.
  • Once the tax return is filed, a copy should be stored in our Dropbox records, and also provided to our grant writer.
  • A copy of the completed tax return must also be provided to the Board of Directors, either by e-mail or as a hard copy at the next board meeting.
